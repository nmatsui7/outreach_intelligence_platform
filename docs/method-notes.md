# Method Notes: Practical AI Adoption and Workflow Intelligence

## Workflow-first AI Adoption

- Start from concrete workflow problems rather than from tool usage alone.
- Look for repeated, annoying, or time-consuming work that staff already understand well.
- Break workflows into steps before deciding whether AI, conventional automation, templates, search, or human judgment is the right support.
- Begin with small workflow improvements that can be tested quickly and reviewed by the people closest to the work.

## Knowledge Sources and Workflow Evidence

- Gather enough workflow evidence to understand how work actually happens before proposing automation.
- In this demo, meeting notes and interaction notes are treated as one safe evidence source because they can be entered manually and reviewed before use.
- Record the knowledge sources that an AI-supported workflow would need, such as policies, templates, prior notes, staff expertise, examples, and quality standards.
- Preserve source references where practical so future users can understand why a workflow opportunity, risk, or failure case was recorded.

## Failure Cases and Human Review

- Document failure cases, edge cases, missing context, and exceptions before treating a workflow as ready for broader reuse.
- Use failure cases to define where human review is required.
- Keep human review explicit for work that is externally visible, quality-sensitive, private, ambiguous, or judgment-heavy.
- Treat AI outputs as drafts, recommendations, or structured notes unless a reviewed process proves that stronger automation is safe.

## Human Systems, Incentives, and Recognition

- AI adoption depends on workload, trust, role clarity, evaluation, recognition, and leadership practices.
- Efficiency gains can create resistance when they only increase staff workload or make expectations unclear.
- Adoption planning should ask whether staff have a reason to share improvements and whether their contributions are recognized.
- Experienced staff should help guide workflow design because they understand quality standards, exceptions, and practical constraints.

## AI Talent and Workflow Learning

- AI talent develops through repeated workflow learning, not only through prompt or tool training.
- Strong adoption practice helps staff identify inefficient work, test small improvements, review results, and share what they learned.
- Internal examples are useful because they make automation possibilities concrete and reusable for similar workflows.
- Fast testing and feedback are more useful than waiting for a perfect first version.

## Knowledge Review and Representation

- Accumulated workflow notes should be reviewed periodically before they are reused as formal guidance.
- Reusable examples should be checked for quality, context, failure cases, and human-review boundaries.
- Reviewed knowledge can later be represented as playbooks, `SKILL.md` instructions, or RAG-style retrieval sources when the evidence is mature enough.
- Informal notes should remain clearly separated from formal research, policy, or endorsed methodology.
